Worlds is committed to fostering a welcoming, professional, and inclusive community. These standards apply to all contributors, maintainers, and users participating in any project space — including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any community channels.

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Be respectful" icon="heart">
    Treat every participant with dignity and kindness. Disagreement is normal — personal attacks are not.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Be professional" icon="briefcase">
    Focus on technical excellence and collaborative problem-solving. Keep discussions on-topic.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Be inclusive" icon="users">
    Welcome contributors of all backgrounds and experience levels. Avoid language or behavior that excludes others.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Be constructive" icon="message-square">
    Give clear, actionable feedback. Critique ideas, not people. Explain your reasoning.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Specific behaviors that contribute to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy toward other community members

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behaviors are not tolerated in any project space:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form
- Sexualized language or imagery
- Personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment of any participant
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Any other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, report it promptly.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Public issues (GitHub)">
    For conduct issues that occur in public GitHub spaces (issues, PRs, discussions), you can flag the specific comment using GitHub's built-in reporting tools, or contact a maintainer directly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Private or sensitive reports">
    For sensitive reports or situations where you are not comfortable reporting publicly, contact the maintainers by email at [support@wazoo.dev](mailto:support@wazoo.dev). Reports are treated confidentially.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

When reporting, include:

- Your contact information (if comfortable sharing)
- The names or usernames of anyone involved
- A description of the incident, including dates, times, and links where applicable
- Any additional context or witnesses

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they determine to be inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

### Enforcement guidelines

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Correction">
    **Community impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other unprofessional behavior.

    **Consequence**: A private written warning from a maintainer explaining the violation and why it was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Warning">
    **Community impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

    **Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Temporary ban">
    **Community impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

    **Consequence**: A temporary ban from any kind of interaction or public communication with the community.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Permanent ban">
    **Community impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression toward an individual or group.

    **Consequence**: A permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces. Representation of a project includes using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an event.
